How to Use This Curriculum

📝

Research Notebook

Start a single document (ml_theory_notes.md) on day 1. Every week record: derivations you worked through, diagrams you drew, insights, and — most importantly — research questions that occur to you. After 12 weeks this becomes the seed for a methods paper.

📄

Weekly Artifact

Each phase lists a short writing deliverable (1-pager, derivation, technical note). Do these. Writing forces real understanding in a way that reading and coding alone don't.

💡

Paper Ideation

Paper ideation starts week 6, not week 12. By the time you've covered transformers, equivariance, and diffusion, you'll have enough vocabulary to start sketching ideas. Capture them in your notebook immediately.

Appendix

Math Refreshers

Consult as needed — don't read front-to-back. Your ChemE PhD covers calculus and linear algebra. These fill specific gaps for ML theory.

Σ Linear Algebra & Matrix Calculus ▾

Σ Group Theory (Phase 2) ▾

Key concepts: group, subgroup, group action, orbit, stabilizer, representation, irreducible representation, equivariant map, invariant map. You do not need full abstract algebra — just these concepts applied to SO(3), SE(3), and permutation groups.

Σ Probability & Information Theory (Phases 3-4) ▾

Key concepts: KL divergence, mutual information, ELBO, reparameterization trick, score function (∇x log p(x)). If you can derive the ELBO from scratch, you have enough probability.

Σ Stochastic Processes & SDEs (Phase 3) ▾

Key concepts: Brownian motion, Ito SDE, Fokker-Planck equation, forward/reverse SDE relationship, probability flow ODE. The last three are the most important for diffusion models.

Σ Optimization (Phase 5) ▾

Key concepts: convexity, duality, KKT conditions (Ch 5), GP prior/posterior, kernel functions, acquisition functions (EI, UCB, Thompson sampling).
